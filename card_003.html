Iberian Lynx

By the turn of the 21st century, the Iberian Lynx was on the verge of extinction, as only about 100 individuals survived in two isolated subpopulations in Andalusia.

  • Scientific Name: Lynx Pardinus
  • Average Lenght: 85-110 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 13 years
  • Habitat: Dense scrub and open pasture

The Iberian Lynx, is a wild cat species native to the Iberian Peninsula in southwestern Europe, that is listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List. It preys almost exclusively on the European rabbit. In the 20th century, the Iberian Lynx population declined because of sharp declines in rabbit populations, caused by myxomatosis, rabbit haemorrhagic disease and overhunting, fragmentation of grassland and forest habitats and poaching.

They are called burrowing owls as they are the only owls in the world that lives in underground burrows.

  • Scientific Name: Athene cunicularia
  • Average Lenght: 10 year
  • Average Weight: 5.7 oz
  • Wingspan: 20 - 24 in.

What they look like: Burrowing Owls are small brown long-legged owls that nest in burrows. They have short tails and no ear tufts. The eyes and bill are yellow.They are a diurnal bird - active both day and night. This is unlike others owls that are mainly active at night.Burrowing Owls often live in loose colonies.
