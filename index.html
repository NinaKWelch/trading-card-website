Sea Otter

Otter grooming includes cheek rubbing and teeth cleaning

  • Scientific Name: Enhydra lutris
  • Average Lenght: 125 cm
  • Average Lifespan: 15 years
  • Habitat: North Pacific coast

Sea Otters are exellent swimmers and spend their days catching fish and teaching their pups handy tricks, like how to use stones to break open shellfish.

Canadian Sphynx

Sphynxes are known for their extroverted behavior.

  • Scientific Name: Felis catus
  • Average Lenght: 6-12 lbs
  • Average Lifespan: 8-14 years
  • Habitat: The human heart

The largely hairless Sphynx is very people-oriented and affectionate. They are highly intelligent, playful, cuddly, often described as being "part dog, part child, part monkey, and part cat". They display a high level of energy, intelligence, curiosity, and affection for their owners

Pikachu

Pikachu is a short, chubby rodent Pokémon. It is covered in yellow fur with two horizontal brown stripes on its back.

  • Name: Pikachu
  • Weight: 6.0kg
  • Height: 0.4m
  • Type: Electric

The anime has shown that Pikachu sometimes travel in groups. It raises its tail to check its surroundings, and is occasionally struck by lightning in this position.

Owl

Most owls share an innate ability to fly almost silently and also more slowly in comparison to other birds of prey.

  • Favoutire food: Insects
  • Average weight: 4.2 kg
  • Oldest Owl: 15 years old
  • Wingspan averages: 140 cm

Owls are enigmatic birds, by turns mysterious, lovable, or spooky, depending on who you ask. With over 200 species living on every continent except Antarctica, owls have super-tuned senses that help them hunt prey all over the world.

Grizzly Bear

Grizzly bears are omnivores and they eat berries, roots, fungi, deer, elk, and fish.

  • Scientific Name: Ursus Arctos Horribilis
  • Average Weight: Up to 360 kg
  • Diet: Large mammals, salmon, plants
  • Habitat: Canada & Northwestern US

The grizzly bear is a large subspecies of brown bear inhabiting North America. Scientists generally do not use the name grizzly bear but call it the North American brown bear

Whippet Dog Breed

Whippets are the fastest domesticated animals of their weight. They can get up to breakneck speeds of 35 mph.

  • Bred For: Coursing, racing
  • Features: Dolichocephalic (long face)
  • Weight Range: Male 25-40 lbs / Female 20-35 lbs
  • Coat: Short

After seeing a Whippet run at full speed on the course or in the field, it’s hard to believe this is the very same dog that curls up on the sofa and binge-watches six hours of TV with you.
This medium-size, graceful sighthound is a laser-focused sprinter when racing or lure coursing and a quiet, gentle companion in the house. It’s almost like getting two dogs in one!

Common leopard gecko
(spotted fat-tailed gecko)

The cutest cricket-eater on Earth!

  • Scientific Name: Eublepharis macularius
  • Average Lenght: 18-28 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Habitat: Highlands

The spotted fat-tailed gecko is a cathemeral, ground-dwelling lizard, naturally found in the highlands of Asia. Leopard geckos make great pet for both the beginner and seasoned reptile caretaker. Extremely cute!

Rico

Rico loves to eat and speaks Greek fluently!

  • Age: 8 months and 11 days
  • Language: Greek
  • Favorite activity: Singing
  • Habitat: Temporibus necessitatibus

The cockatiel (Nymphicus hollandicus), also known as the quarrion and the weiro, is a bird that is a member of the cockatoo family endemic to Australia.

Ozzie(my dog)

During this summer a wire from my electric box started smelling awkward but I did not notice till Ozzie managed to make me know that there was something wrong. If it was not for his help during that day my house would have burned down. Is strange to see how a dog so small can save your life in a hard situation.

  • Diet: He eats all sort of things from Ice cream to Beef
  • Attitude: He loves everyone
  • Toys: Balls mostly but also he has a little Teddy bear
  • Friends: All the possible dogs but also all the friendly humans

The Bichon Frise (pronounced BEE-shawn FREE-say; the plural is Bichons Frises) is a cheerful, small dog breed with a love of mischief and a lot of love to give. With his black eyes and fluffy white coat, the Bichon looks almost like a child’s toy. And it doesn’t take long to realize that the Bichon can be your happiest and most enthusiastic companion.
